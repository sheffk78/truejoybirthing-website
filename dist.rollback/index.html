Your Questions About Birth Plans, Answered

The things first-time moms ask us most, answered honestly.

What is a birth plan, and do I really need one?

A birth plan is a document that spells out your preferences for labor, delivery, and postpartum care. It's not a rigid checklist — it's a communication tool that helps your medical team know what matters to you. The free Joyful Birth Plan covers every stage and works in your real hospital room.

Can I fill out a birth plan on my phone?

Yes! The True Joy Birthing app lets you build your birth plan step by step on your phone. Nine guided sections walk you through hospital preferences, pain management, and more — and you can update it anytime. Prefer the fillable PDF? Download it free here.

What if my doctor recommends something I didn't expect?

That's exactly what a birth plan is for. It gives you the words when your doctor recommends something you hadn't thought about. The Guided Birth Plan Walkthrough free video series covers what to say in these moments, and the 1:1 Confidence Session lets you practice the conversation.

How do I choose between the PDF and the app?

Both are free! The app is best if you want step-by-step guidance, the ability to update your plan anytime, and share it with your care team. The PDF is best if you want a printable one-pager your nurse can read in two minutes. Many families use both — build in the app, print the PDF for the hospital bag.

Is there a doula or midwife directory?

Yes — use the True Joy Birthing app to find doulas and midwives in your area. You can also browse our city-by-city guides for local hospital info, doula costs, and Medicaid status.
