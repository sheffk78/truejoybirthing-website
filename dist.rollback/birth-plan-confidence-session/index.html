What happens in a Birth Plan Confidence Session

You and a certified birth doula sit down on Zoom with your completed birth plan and go through it together — section by section, line by line.

This is not a lecture. It's a working session. You bring your plan, your questions, and the things you're unsure about. We bring the birth-room experience to make sure your plan reflects what actually happens — not just what the brochure says.

  • Line-by-line review of YOUR birth plan — not a template, not generic advice. Your filled-out plan, your hospital, your concerns.
  • Advocacy role-play — practice what to say when your provider suggests something you didn't plan for. "I need a minute to think," "What happens if we wait?," "Can you explain the reasoning?"
  • Walk away confident — you'll know what to communicate, when to say it, and why.

What we cover

01 — Labor and delivery interventions

What providers are likely to recommend, what they actually mean, and how to say yes, no, or "I need a minute" with confidence. We review your preferences for monitoring, IV, movement, induction, and augmentation — and flag gaps where the hospital's default might override what you wrote.

02 — Pain relief preferences

Not just "epidural or unmedicated." We walk through the full pain management ladder — what's available, when it's typically offered, and how to keep your options open at each stage. You'll leave with language that doesn't box you in.

03 — Post-delivery and newborn procedures

Skin-to-skin, cord clamping, golden hour, feeding, Vitamin K, Hep B, eye ointment, circumcision, rooming-in, screenings. These decisions come fast after delivery. We make sure your plan covers them clearly — and that your support team knows them cold.

04 — Advocating with your provider

This is the heart of the session. We role-play the conversations you're most worried about — induction talks, epidural timing, cesarean discussions, anything that feels like pressure. You practice the phrases, we sharpen them, and you walk away knowing what to say when the moment comes.

05 — Plan B, Plan C, and flexibility language

Birth doesn't follow scripts. We help you write backup language into your plan so that if things shift — and they often do — you still have a voice. This is where most birth plans fall apart. We make sure yours doesn't.

Why it matters

You've done the research. You've downloaded the plan. You've watched the videos. But knowing your options and speaking up in the moment are two different things.

Feel prepared, not just informed. There's a difference between reading about induction and having practiced what you'll say when your OB brings it up at 39 weeks. We bridge that gap.

Have language that works in the room. "I prefer" sounds different than "I'd like to understand the reasoning before we decide." We help you find the words that get heard — not just said.

Give your partner a script, not a spotlight. Your support person wants to help but may not know what to say. Your plan gives them the words. Your session makes sure those words actually work when a nurse has three other patients and your doctor is recommending Pitocin.

Walk in knowing what you'll say when plans change. Most births don't follow the plan. That's not failure — that's reality. A good birth plan includes language for flexibility. A great one includes language for the conversations that happen when flexibility becomes the plan.

Frequently asked questions

What do I need before the session?

A completed or near-complete birth plan and a quiet space for Zoom. Download the free Joyful Birth Plan if you don't have one yet — even a rough draft gives us something to work with.

How long is the session?

60–75 minutes. Long enough to go through your plan in detail and practice advocacy scenarios. Short enough that you don't need to block off your whole day.

Is this medical advice?

No. Shelbi is a certified birth doula, not a medical provider. This is education and advocacy coaching — helping you understand your options and communicate them clearly. Always discuss your specific medical situation with your OB, midwife, or care team.

Can I book more than one session?

You can, but most parents find one is enough. The session is designed to give you everything you need to walk into your birth with confidence.

What if I haven't filled out a birth plan yet?

Start with the free Joyful Birth Plan and fill out as much as you can. If you're truly starting from zero, the Guided Birth Plan Walkthrough is a great place to build your first draft before the session.

Is this only for Texas residents?

No — sessions are on Zoom and available nationwide. Shelbi is based in Texas but serves expectant parents across the United States.

Can my partner join?

Yes. Partners are welcome and encouraged. Many families find it helpful for both people to hear the review and practice the language together. No extra charge.

Is this only for hospital births?

No. We work with families planning hospital births, birth center births, and home births. The session is tailored to your specific setting and provider. That said, hospital-birth families tend to have the most advocacy questions — and those are the conversations we're best equipped to practice.

What's the cancellation policy?

Life happens, especially when you're pregnant. You can reschedule up to 24 hours before your session with no penalty. No-shows and late cancellations are non-refundable.

Is this covered by insurance or Medicaid?

We cannot bill insurance or Medicaid directly. Some families have been reimbursed for doula education through their HSA/FSA or state Medicaid doula benefit — check with your plan administrator. We provide a receipt you can submit.
