What You'll Find on Each City Page

Every city page is a complete birth planning resource built for that specific location — not a generic template. Here's what's inside.

Doulas & Midwives Directory

Every city page lists the doulas and midwives serving that area — with their credentials, services offered, cost ranges, and a direct link to their practice. You can see who's accepting new clients, what they specialize in, and whether they work with your insurance. No directory listing is ever behind a paywall or login.

Hospital & Birth Center Info

We list every major hospital in the area with its NICU level, so you know which facilities can handle your needs. Birth centers are listed separately with their own details. Each hospital page links to our free birth plan template so you can walk in prepared.

Real Cost Information

Doula costs vary wildly by city — from $800 in smaller markets to $5,000+ in major metros. We show the real range for your city so you know what to expect. No hidden fees, no "contact for pricing" runaround. We also explain what your insurance might cover and how to ask your plan the right questions.

Medicaid & Insurance Guidance

Every city page includes state-specific Medicaid information — whether doula services are covered, how to enroll, and what the reimbursement rates are. We also explain how to use a superbill for out-of-network reimbursement if your private insurance doesn't cover doula care directly.

City Guide Videos

Many city pages include a short video guide walking you through the hospitals, doulas, costs, and insurance options for that specific city. These are narrated by Shelbi, a certified birth doula, and give you a quick overview before you dive into the details. New videos are added every week.

Local Birth Culture & FAQs

Every city has its own birth culture — the hospitals families trust, the doulas who've been there for decades, the community organizations doing the work. We capture that local context so you're not reading generic advice. Each page also answers the questions real families ask about that city, from NICU levels to VBAC policies.

Why a City-Specific Birth Guide Matters

Pregnancy is overwhelming enough without having to piece together local information from a dozen different websites. Hospital policies vary by state. Doula costs range from $800 to $5,000 depending on where you live. Medicaid covers doula care in some states but not others. And the doula who's perfect for a home birth in Portland might not be the right fit for a hospital birth in Dallas.

That's why every city page on True Joy Birthing is built specifically for that location. We research the hospitals, the doulas, the costs, and the insurance landscape so you don't have to. Whether you're planning a hospital birth in New York City, a birth center experience in Denver, or a home birth with a midwife in Portland, you'll find the information you need in one place — written in plain language, not medical jargon.

And because birth plans aren't one-size-fits-all, the free True Joy Birthing app lets you build a plan that matches your specific preferences, then share it with your provider or doula. The app is free for every family — no subscriptions, no hidden costs, no account required to start building.
